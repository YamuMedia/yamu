Key Takeaways — Full Analysis
Pinning is hurting efficiency across multiple headlines. Pinned versions consistently show 2–6x higher CPA than their unpinned counterparts. "Bot-free AI Meeting Notetaker" pinned = $235.61 CPA vs "Now With Bot-free, For Free" unpinned = $119.06 CPA. "#1 Rated AI Notetaker" pinned = $280.94 CPA. "Fathom: AI Meeting Assistant" pinned = $166.58 vs unpinned variants well below target. Audit every pinned headline and remove pins where the unpinned equivalent is more efficient. See Search Headlines tab.
"Never Take Notes Again" is exhausted across both surfaces. $357.09 CPA in Search (5.42 conv, $1,935 spend) and LOW label in PMax. This is one of the oldest angles in the account and the data confirms fatigue. Scale down in Search and queue for PMax replacement.
"AI-Powered Meeting Notes" is misread by PMax — protect it in Search. Both PMax campaigns assign it LOW, which typically signals removal. But in Search RSAs it is running at $20.71 CPA with 16.5 conv and 18.66% CTR — top-3 efficiency in the account. PMax and Search use different signals to assign labels. Do not remove this headline from Search RSAs based on PMax label alone. See Search Headlines tab.
Competitors campaign needs a full reset, not a copy refresh. CPA degraded from $98 to $233 over the period. The underlying issue is copy-to-query mismatch. The highest-converting terms are "fathom vs fireflies" (2 conv, $145 CPA) and "similar to otter ai" (1 conv, $145 CPA) — both intent-specific queries. Current copy uses generic switcher language. New copy should speak directly to those specific comparisons, not switcher intent in general. See Keyword Details tab.
Bot-free is validated on every surface — the label drops in US PMax are execution-level, not signal-level. Campaign CVR = 2.53% (highest non-branded search). "Now With Bot-free, For Free" = $119.06 CPA / 31.28 conv in Search. Bot-free descriptions collectively = 85+ conv across the period. The two descriptions that dropped to LOW in US PMax are isolated to that asset pool. The messaging is working. Rotate the specific executions, not the strategy.
Speed and effort-reduction angles are the most efficient copy type in the account — and the most under-exposed. "Notes Sent Before You Blink" ($70.45 CPA), "Skip Notes. Remember More." ($67.30), "No More Manual Note-Taking" ($46.68), "Focus on Meetings, Not Notes" ($61.40), "Sign Up & Save Hours on Notes" ($55.78) — all below target, all low impression volume. These are getting fewer impressions than "Get Started. Free Forever." ($136 CPA) which has 61k impressions. Budget is skewing toward volume anchors rather than efficiency winners. See Search Headlines tab.
"Accurate meeting notes & action items delivered to your inbox" graduated from zero to standout. Showed 0 conv in the 7-day May 18–24 snapshot. Full-period data shows 8.42 conv at $81.94 CPA — best CPA among named-action descriptions. This is a direct signal that the inbox-delivery angle works over longer attribution windows. Expand exposure. See Search Descriptions tab.
