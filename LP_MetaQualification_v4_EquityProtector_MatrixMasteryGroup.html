FAQ

Common questions.

Is this just more credit cards? I get offers in the mail every week.
The cards that arrive in the mail are consumer-facing products with $3K–$10K limits from retail bank divisions. The products in MMG's sequence are business credit lines from Chase, Amex, US Bank, and Wells Fargo commercial divisions, in the $20K–$75K range per line. The same institutions, a different product class entirely.
Will this show up on my personal credit or affect my HELOC?
Business credit lines structured through this process do not appear on personal credit reports in most cases. They do not interfere with personal mortgage refinancing or HELOC applications for most clients. We'll confirm the specific answer for your profile on the qualification call, before any fee is discussed.
My bank already declined me. Does that change my chances here?
Likely not. Banks evaluate business credit against W-2 or Schedule C documentation. The lenders MMG works with evaluate your credit score, your entity structure, and your utilization rate. A bank decline based on self-employment income structure doesn't reflect how these lenders see your profile.
How much can I actually expect to access?
The range comes from your actual credit file on the call. Typical range for qualified business owner profiles is $50K–$300K in a first round, depending on credit score, utilization, and entity setup. You'll get a specific projection before any payment.
How is this different from capital stacking services I've already seen?
The structural differences: a flat fee instead of a percentage of what you fund; lenders named on the call before you sign, not after; and the same consultants who set up your stack are still reachable in year three. The BBB record is public and year-over-year. Check it before you book.
What happens after the 0% intro period?
MMG builds the rotation plan before the first round closes. The 0% period runs 9–21 months per lender. Before expiry, we sequence new applications to keep the structure active. Year two renewal is $2,500. The system is built to keep working past the first intro period.
Is there a long-term contract?
No. The program fee covers the full advisory process. After year one, a $2,500 annual renewal applies. No contract beyond the initial engagement. CROA three-day cooling-off period applies from the date of signing.
